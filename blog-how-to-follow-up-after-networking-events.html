How to Follow Up After Networking Events: The Complete Guide

The conversation at the event is just the beginning. Here's how to turn those connections into lasting business relationships.

You just left a great networking event. You met 15 interesting people, had meaningful conversations, and collected a stack of business cards. Now what?

The difference between people who build powerful networks and those who don't isn't the number of events they attend — it's what they do in the 48 hours after.

The 48-Hour Follow-Up Framework

Research from Harvard Business Review shows that the probability of converting a networking connection into a business relationship drops by 50% after 48 hours. Here's the framework top networkers use:

Hour 0-2: Capture While It's Fresh

Before you leave the venue (or on the ride home), document every contact:

  • Name and company — the basics
  • What you discussed — the key topic, their pain point, or the mutual interest
  • What you promised — "I'll send you that article" or "Let me intro you to Sarah"
  • Personal details — their dog's name, the vacation they mentioned, their kid's school
Pro tip: Use a networking app like Network Pro to capture contacts in under 10 seconds. The AI automatically enriches each contact with their job title, LinkedIn profile, and company details — so you can focus on the personal context that makes your follow-up memorable.

Hour 2-24: Personalized First Touch

Send a brief, personalized email to your top 5-10 contacts. The key word is personalized. Generic "nice to meet you" emails get ignored. Reference something specific from your conversation.

Subject: The [specific topic] we discussed at [event name]

Hi [Name],

Great meeting you at [event] yesterday. I really enjoyed our conversation about [specific topic].

[Deliver on your promise: "Here's the article I mentioned" or "I'd love to connect you with Sarah — she's dealt with the exact same challenge."]

Would love to continue the conversation. Are you free for a 15-minute call next week?

Best,
[Your name]

Hour 24-48: LinkedIn + Second Tier

Connect on LinkedIn with a personalized note (not the default). Follow up with your second-tier contacts — people you had shorter conversations with but who are still relevant to your network.

The 5 Follow-Up Mistakes That Kill Relationships

1. Waiting Too Long

After 48 hours, the other person has met dozens more people and your conversation is fading. Speed wins.

2. Being Generic

"It was great meeting you!" is forgettable. "Your insight about using AI to qualify leads faster was eye-opening" is memorable.

3. Asking Too Soon

Don't pitch your product in the first follow-up. Give value first — share a resource, make an introduction, offer genuine help.

4. Following Up Once and Stopping

The fortune is in the follow-up. Most valuable relationships require 3-5 touchpoints. Set reminders to reconnect at natural intervals.

5. Not Tracking Your Network

If you can't find someone's details 3 months later when they become relevant, the connection is wasted. Use a system.
